NASA

challenge
revamp NASA's logo to reflect their evolving brand.

solution
First was the research into NASA and what they do to have a better understanding of the agency and what the new logo should look like. Perhaps many people do not realize this or they do not care, but many things everyday people see come from NASA (see: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA_spinoff_technologies and https://spinoff.nasa.gov/Spinoff2008/tech_benefits.html). I wanted the outworks of the logo to represent a gear in reference to inventions and technology, as well as having the shape of a human eye to put it's focus NASA's missions-- space exploration and helping mankind. The teeth at the side of the gear are shorter than the rest to put emphasis on the middle of the logo or the inside of the gear. Inside the gear represents an abstract galaxy and star to signify space with the star as a iris to the eye-shaped gear. I wanted the colors not to be to be too bright and not the usual selection of colors people pick for space, thus Pantone 669 EC (purple) for peace, ambition, explorations and galaxies while Pantone 143 EC (yellow/orange) for energy and knowledge or awareness. Considering Avenir means "future," it seemed like the best choice to pick it for typography.
